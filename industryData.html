How has the Burning Glass Data affected your opinion of your ideal job?

Jacopo

Front End Developers and Web Designers are two of the most requested IT Job titles, with the former ranking sixth and the latter ranking second according to the Burning Glass 2018 report. This made me more confident about my goals and career decisions. Although I was not surprised to learn that communication and problem solving skills are the two general skills in greatest demand, I was not expecting decision making and management skills to be among the last ones. This shows how important it is to both being able to work in a team and by yourself in the IT industry. As for the most requested IT skills, seeing JAVA and JavaScript ranking second and third highlighted how fundamental programming skills are for the job I want to do.

Nicholas

After reviewing the 2018 Burning Glass Industry report for IT within Australia, it is good to see that Front-End Web Developers are highly sought-after within the IT industry. It is also interesting to see that the most in-demand specialised skills are SQL and JavaScript. Although it may not be required for a Front End Developer, it might be worth looking into adding SQL into my skill-set, as it may create further opportunities within the industry. Although, it is not surprising to me that communication was the top baseline skill required in IT, it really confirms to me how important good communication is to succeed within the industry.

Jeremy

Service Technician is a highly rated job title for employers within Australia. I think that it is very encouraging to know that they are looking for my ideal job. It's interesting to see that communication skills are so highly rated in general skills. I think that it is slightly scary to see that programming languages are so highly rated in IT skills, but it is also not surprising. I really need to start looking into the programming languages and integrate them into my skills. At the moment I am not doing the introduction to programming due to other commitments, but I am going to enrol for the next study period and am very excited to start looking more deeply into programming languages.

Adam

According to the Burning Glass report from 2018, Software Developers are one of the most sought-after professions in the industry. Including job listing for Software Engineers, Developers, & their senior counterparts, it is ranked #1 for active selections. This, along with 2 of the general skills with the most demand being communication skills and problem solving, makes me extremely confident about my ideal job. Customer service, technical support and graphic design being in the middle-rankings for specialised skills (although my graphic design skills need work) also improves my confidence in my ability to find work in the IT field. To be honest, I expected team building and analytical skills to be further up on the rankings of general skills, and I also believe project management being one of the highest specialised skills in demand means I may have to work more on some of my weaknesses.

Hamilton

My decision has not changed at all after reviewing the industry data, I can see how the Cyber/information security engineer/analyst had 2,914 vacancies form March 01, 2017 to February 28,2018 which when dealing with the security of peoples and organisations data storage and transmission seems like too much, this is one of the reasons why this job appeals to me.

I found it very interesting how communication skills were by far the highest sought-after skill defiantly something to keep in mind and build upon if I want to carve a successful path for myself in the industry. I was glad to see problem solving, organisational skills and creativity around the top, all skills I believe I possess. Most of the top specialised skills in demand I at least have some knowledge of apart from SQL, (which happens to be the most in demand), I do think I’m on the right track when the most in demand skills in the industry are ones I’m becoming familiar with.
